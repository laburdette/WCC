Marie Curie

"Nothing in life is to be feared, it is only to be understood. Now is the time to understand more, so that we may fear less."

Marie Curie was born in Warsaw on November 7, 1867, born to a secondary-school teacher. She received a general education in local schools and some scientific training from her father. She studied at the Sorbonne in Paris, where she met her husband, Pierre Curie who was working in physics. She is most famous for her work in discovering how to isolate radium and polonium after the discovery of radioactivity. She and her husband also recieved the Nobel Prize in physics for their work together, and after Pierre pased away, she continued to do work with her daughter as well as write for several journals and schools.

Interesting Facts

  1. Only person to win Nobel Prizes in both physics and chemistry
  2. She graduated high school at age 15
  3. Marie was the first person to be awarded two Nobel Prizes
  4. The Curie Institute in Paris was founded by Marie to further her interest in cancer research
